## Hi there, I am Vishal

- 🔭 I am a PhD student in Pure Mathematics at Memorial University of Newfoundland.
- 🌱 My background includes several years of teaching mathematics, curriculum development for accessibility-focused education, and a machine learning project on predicting hospital readmissions. Working on applied, data-driven problems is what drew me toward data science and machine learning.


## Experience

### Teaching
- **Guest Faculty** at **Deen Dayal Upadhyaya College**  

- **Guest Faculty** at **University of Delhi** 
  
- **Teaching Assistant** at **Memorial University of Newfoundland**  

### Curriculum Developer
- **Curriculum Developer, Accessive Learning for K–2 Students**  
  Contributed to a fully funded initiative by the Learning Disabilities Association of Newfoundland and Labrador aimed at improving learning outcomes for children with disabilities through technology integration. Responsibilities included researching evidence-based instructional methods and helping design a research-informed curriculum.

### Data Science / Machine Learning
- **Data Science Project: Predicting 30-Day Hospital Readmissions (Sep–Dec 2025)**  
  Developed a machine-learning framework using a synthetic EHR dataset spanning demographics, encounters, procedures, conditions, medications, and observations. Compared interpretable and ensemble models to identify high-risk patients and support more targeted post-discharge care.

### Mentoring
- **Mentor, Erdos Institute**  
  Mentored a data science team working on sleep staging and forecasting. I supported the team in shaping the project design, modeling approach, and evaluation framework.

## Research

I work in **algebra**, especially **Lie theory**, **Lie algebra gradings**, **algebraic groups**, and **combinatorial game theory**. My recent research includes work on **misère games**, **Kac’s classification theorem in prime characteristic**, and **fine gradings on** \(E_6\).

### Papers
- *Invertibility in the Misère Multiverse* — accepted in the *Electronic Journal of Combinatorics*.
- *On the Reductions and Expansions of Misère Games* — in preparation.
- *Fine Gradings on \(E_6\)* — in preparation.
